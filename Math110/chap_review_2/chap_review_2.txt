<hr><b>General Procedure for Finding the Equation of a Tangent Line:</b><br>
In order to find the equation of the line tangent to the curve \[y = f(x)\] at the point \[(a,b)\], it's enough to find the slope of the curve at \[x = a\].  To do so, compute \[f'(x)\] and then plug in \[x = a\].  The slope of the tangent line is \[f'(a)\].  Therefore, using the point-slope equation of a line, the equation of the tangent line can be written as
<br><br><center>y - b = f'(a)(x-a)</center>
